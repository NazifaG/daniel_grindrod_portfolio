ABOUT ME

Dr Gillian Jack

I’m a historian of late medieval and early modern
Italy, with a focus on prostitution, nuns, and
welfare. My doctoral thesis was on Sant’Elisabetta
delle Convertite, a monastery for repentant
prostitutes in Florence.

My research focuses on poor women and girls in
fifteenth- and sixteenth-century Florence. I am
interested in how they negotiated their
interactions with the city and how civic authorities
attempted both to restrict and facilitate their lives.

I currently work for The Open University. I am an
Associate Lecturer, teaching history from the
Greek myths to 1975. I am also an Honorary
Associate in the History department.
